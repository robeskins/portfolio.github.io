    • Dissertation titled Nuclear Magnetic Resonance Imaging Techniques:

      • In this project I set out to research the process of forming an image using Nuclear Magnetic Resonance(NMR). Firstly, I set out to explain the fundamentals of NMR and how a signal can be detected when a nuclei is placed in a magnetic field and pulsed with a specific radio wave. This is explained using both a quantum interpretation and a classical interpretation. Once this was explained, I could go onto explaining the spin-spin relaxation time and the spin-lattice relaxation time. Secondly, I went through the early history of MRI imaging and how an image can be formed from the signal given off by nuclei as they were put into an excited state and how a slice can be imaged in the body using gradient magnetic fields. The last fundamental concept of imaging was to explain why we record in K-Space and take a 2D Fourier transform to then get an image from the patient. Thirdly, with the use of K-Space, techniques were researched to increase scan time and resolution. This was due to the social impact of faster scans and more detail. Attached is the research paper.
